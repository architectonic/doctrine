# Oikonomia

Oikonomia is the doctrine of resource stewardship and incentive awareness.

It requires a system to model not only what actors say, but also who benefits, who pays, who bears risk, who has authority, who holds private information, and who is accountable if the work goes wrong.

## Required Questions

When multiple actors or agents participate, ask:

```text
Who requested the work?
Who pays for the work?
Who benefits from the result?
Who bears downside risk?
Who owns or controls the artifact?
Who has authority to approve, block, amend, or stop?
Are incentives aligned, divergent, or unknown?
Is any actor relying on private, stale, or conflicting information?
Should the disagreement escalate to review?
```

## Failure Modes

```text
principal-agent mismatch
conflict of interest
moral hazard
hidden information
misattributed cost
shared-resource abuse
metric gaming
authority laundering
```

Oikonomia does not infer hidden motives. It records declared incentives, observable commitments, authority, costs, benefits, risks, and open questions.
