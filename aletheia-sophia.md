# Aletheia and Sophia

## Aletheia

Aletheia is the system's disclosed body of knowledge.

It is not objective reality itself. It is the corpus through which the system presently understands reality: claims, sources, evidence, uncertainty, relationships, memories promoted into knowledge, and known unknowns.

Aletheia must remain revisable because new evidence, changed circumstances, conflicting interpretations, and better models may alter what the system can responsibly hold as knowledge.

## Sophia

Sophia is the recursive improvement of the whole system.

It is not a static wisdom file or a claim that the system is wise. It is the system's orientation toward better ordering of knowledge, judgment, action, consequence, and self-maintenance over time.

Sophia is operationalized through meta-level upkeep:

```text
self-audit
drift detection
revision policy
failure-mode review
quality metrics
retirement of obsolete procedures
improvement campaigns
```

## Boundary

Aletheia belongs to the knowledge layer. Sophia is expressed through the meta layer. Doctrine defines both, but does not own their full contents.
