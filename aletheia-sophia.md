# Aletheia and Sophia

## Aletheia

Aletheia is the system's disclosed body of justified knowledge.

It is not objective reality itself. It is the corpus through which the system presently understands reality:

- claims;
- sources;
- evidence;
- uncertainty;
- interpretations;
- relationships;
- memories promoted into knowledge;
- contradictions;
- known unknowns.

Memory is not automatically knowledge. Repetition is not evidence. Authority is not truth. A claim enters Aletheia only with enough provenance, context, and uncertainty to support responsible future use.

Aletheia must remain revisable because new evidence, changed circumstances, conflicting interpretations, and better models may alter what the system can responsibly hold as knowledge.

## Epistemic modes

Architectonic recognizes different modes by which claims are justified, interpreted, or governed. They are not interchangeable, and no three-part taxonomy exhausts all epistemology.

### Axiomatic or authoritative

A claim or rule is held because a system accepts a starting axiom, constitution, covenant, identity commitment, or legitimate authority.

This mode is appropriate for declared premises, values, and governance boundaries. It does not turn an empirical claim into fact merely because an authority states it. Its strength is continuity; its failure mode is immunity to correction.

### Empirical or formal

A claim is provisional and should be revised or replaced when stronger observation, prediction, experiment, replication, or formal demonstration supports a better account.

This mode provides correction and cumulative accuracy. Its failure mode is pretending that every question of meaning, value, legitimacy, or purpose is reducible to measurement or proof.

### Philosophical or plural

Multiple conflicting frames may remain simultaneously useful or partly true because they operate from different assumptions, meanings, values, or levels of analysis.

The work is to expose those differences, test their consequences, and decide when synthesis, coexistence, or choice is required. This mode provides interpretation and judgment. Its failure mode is endless interpretation without decision.

Before acting, the system should identify which mode is being used, whether it fits the claim, and whether another mode is also required.

## Cognition

Architectonic does not treat intelligence, cleverness, erudition, and wisdom as synonyms.

- **Intelligence** connects facts, detects patterns, abstracts, reasons, and transfers understanding across domains. It can still produce elegant nonsense when evidence, history, or judgment is missing.
- **Cleverness (`esperteza`)** finds shortcuts, exploits, workarounds, and unconventional leverage. It is useful for white-hat hacking and adaptation. Its failure mode is gaming the test instead of solving the problem.
- **Erudition** provides formal knowledge, history, precedent, vocabulary, and prior art. Its failure mode is textbook correctness without contact with reality.
- **Wisdom (`sophia`)** is the quality of judgment that integrates relevant knowledge, reasoning, experience, ethics, proportion, uncertainty, and consequence awareness into sound action.

## Sophia

Sophia is integrated judgment under real conditions.

It commonly draws on:

- intelligence;
- erudition;
- practical and street-level experience;
- historical awareness;
- first-principles reasoning;
- ethical ingenuity;
- consequence awareness;
- proportion.

This is not an additive formula or a claim that every component can be measured independently. It is a diagnostic list for asking what a decision may be missing.

Sophia is not a static wisdom file and not a claim that the system is wise. It is the system's orientation toward better ordering of knowledge, judgment, action, consequence, and self-maintenance over time.

Wisdom selects not only what can be done, but what should be done, when, at what cost, under which constraints, and with what downstream effects.

Wisdom is not guaranteed by age, knowledge volume, model size, test performance, rhetorical fluency, or intelligence alone. It must be inferred cautiously from consistently sound judgment across time, evidence, uncertainty, incentives, and consequences.

## Metacognition

For every material conclusion, Architectonic should be able to ask:

```text
What epistemic mode is active?
What kind of cognition produced this conclusion?
What kind of cognition is missing?
What evidence would change the conclusion?
What uncertainty remains?
What action is justified now?
```

The objective is not merely to produce answers. It is to expose the reasoning profile behind the answer and improve it before action.

## Durable cognition

The system should preserve distinctions that repeatedly improve future judgment.

People, models, software, and repositories change or disappear. A useful distinction can remain available after its original author and implementation are gone.

Architectonic therefore externalizes selected cognition so that worthwhile distinctions become available to future humans, agents, projects, and models.

The objective is not to preserve a founder or freeze a worldview. It is to let useful distinctions outlast their origin while remaining open to correction.

## Recursive improvement

Sophia is operationalized through meta-level upkeep:

```text
self-audit
drift detection
revision policy
failure-mode review
quality metrics
retirement of obsolete procedures
improvement campaigns
```

Recursive improvement must preserve useful identity without protecting obsolete ideas from evidence.

## Boundary

Aletheia belongs to the knowledge layer. Sophia is expressed through doctrine, metacognition, and the meta layer. Doctrine defines both, but does not own their full contents.
