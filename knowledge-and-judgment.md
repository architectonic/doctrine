# Knowledge and Judgment

Knowledge is a disclosed, revisable account made from claims, sources, evidence, uncertainty, interpretations, contradictions, and known unknowns. It is not reality itself.

Before material action, identify the form of justification, the reasoning used, the reasoning still missing, the evidence that would change the conclusion, the uncertainty that remains, and the action justified now.

Stored knowledge must remain attributable, challengeable, revisable, and removable.
